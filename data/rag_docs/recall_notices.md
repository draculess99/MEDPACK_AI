## RECALL-2024-001: Foley Catheters — Balloon Deflation Defect

**Date Issued:** April 15, 2024  
**FDA Recall Class:** Class II  
**Affected Product:** Foley Catheters  
**Affected Lot Numbers:** #88392, #88393, #88401  
**Manufacturer:** Cardinal Health  

### Issue

The retention balloon in affected Foley Catheters may deflate unexpectedly during use, leading to catheter migration, patient discomfort, and potential urethral trauma.

### Required Actions

1. **Immediately quarantine** all units matching the affected lot numbers.
2. Remove affected units from all Pyxis machines, supply carts, and bedside stock.
3. Mark affected inventory as `recalled` in the MedPack AI inventory system.
4. **Do not use** affected units under any circumstances.
5. Notify all departments that routinely use Foley Catheters: ICU, ED, Med-Surg, OR.
6. Contact Cardinal Health for replacement stock and credit (see SLA-003).

### Substitution Guidance

- Use straight catheters for intermittent catheterization if continuous drainage is not required.
- For patients requiring continuous drainage, use an alternate manufacturer's Foley Catheter if available.
- Document the substitution in the patient's chart and in MedPack AI scan events.

## RECALL-2024-002: Telemetry Monitoring Leads — False Asystole Alarms

**Date Issued:** January 8, 2024  
**FDA Recall Class:** Class I (Most Serious)  
**Affected Product:** Telemetry Monitoring Leads  
**Affected Lot Numbers:** #441, #442, #443  
**Manufacturer:** Vendor X (CardioTech Medical)  

### Issue

Affected telemetry leads produce intermittent false asystole (flatline) alarms due to a connector defect. This can lead to unnecessary code-blue activations, alarm fatigue, and delayed response to actual cardiac events.

### Required Actions

1. **Remove immediately** from the ED, ICU, and all monitored units.
2. Replace with Vendor Y (PhilipsConnect) monitoring leads.
3. Audit all telemetry devices in the hospital for affected lots.
4. Report any patient incidents related to this defect to the FDA MedWatch program.
5. Update MedPack AI inventory to mark affected lots as `recalled`.

### Clinical Impact

- **High priority** — false alarms in cardiac monitoring can be life-threatening.
- Assign dedicated staff to manually verify cardiac rhythm for any patient who was monitored with affected leads in the past 30 days.

## RECALL-2024-003: Exam Gloves — Pinhole Defect

**Date Issued:** March 22, 2024  
**FDA Recall Class:** Class II  
**Affected Product:** Nitrile Exam Gloves (Large)  
**Affected Lot Numbers:** #NGL-2024-07 through #NGL-2024-12  
**Manufacturer:** SafeHands Medical  

### Issue

Quality testing revealed a higher-than-acceptable pinhole rate (AQL exceeded 1.5) in affected lots, increasing the risk of barrier failure during patient contact.

### Required Actions

1. Quarantine all affected boxes.
2. Do not distribute to clinical areas.
3. Substitute with Latex Exam Gloves (check patient latex allergy status) or alternate nitrile brand.
4. Contact SafeHands Medical for replacement and credit.

## RECALL-2024-004: IV Extension Sets — Luer Lock Failure

**Date Issued:** June 1, 2024  
**FDA Recall Class:** Class II  
**Affected Product:** IV Extension Sets (72-inch)  
**Affected Lot Numbers:** #EXT-72-501 through #EXT-72-520  
**Manufacturer:** B. Braun Medical  

### Issue

The male Luer lock connector on affected extension sets may not seat properly, causing intermittent disconnections during gravity or pump-driven infusions.

### Required Actions

1. Remove affected lots from all crash carts, IV start kits, and supply rooms.
2. Inspect any in-use extension sets from affected lots — replace immediately if found.
3. Use extension sets from alternate lots or alternate manufacturers.
4. Report to B. Braun for replacement stock.

### Patient Safety Note

- Disconnections during vasopressor or antibiotic infusions can have serious clinical consequences.
- Prioritize replacement in ICU and ED settings.

## General Recall Response Protocol

1. Upon receipt of any FDA recall notice, the Materials Management team must:
   - Identify all affected inventory within **2 hours**.
   - Quarantine affected stock within **4 hours**.
   - Notify all affected departments within **8 hours**.
   - Update MedPack AI inventory status within **12 hours**.
2. The Risk Management team must:
   - Assess patient exposure within **24 hours**.
   - File MedWatch reports as required.
   - Document the response in the facility's recall log.
3. The Supply Chain team must:
   - Contact the manufacturer for replacement and credit.
   - Identify and deploy substitute products.
   - Monitor substitute stock levels for the next **72 hours**.
